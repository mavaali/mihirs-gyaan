# Rubric — TEMPLATE (build your own)

Copy this file to `references/rubric.md` and fill it in. Your populated `rubric.md` stays
**local** (gitignored) — it encodes your voice and your calibration pieces.

The loop in `SKILL.md` is universal. This rubric is the part that makes it good. **Do not use
someone else's rubric directly** — the dimensions and anchors must be yours, or the loop just
optimizes toward a voice that isn't.

See [rubric.waglesworld-blog.md](./rubric.waglesworld-blog.md) for a complete worked example
(blog/essay genre, one author's voice).

## How to build it (≈30 minutes)

1. **Pick 3–5 of your own pieces** (in this genre) that represent your voice when it's working.
2. **Read them as a critic.** What structural moves recur? What carries the argument? What
   language are you allergic to in other people's writing?
3. **Write your dimensions.** Use as many as match what you found — 6, 9, 12. Each scores 1–5.
4. **Set the calibration anchors.** Quote or link your own pieces as the 5/5 reference.
5. **List your anti-patterns** — your specific allergies (these are hard reverts).

The methodology transfers. The specific rubric does not.
