# Exercises — build your confidence

Five small wins, in order, each slightly harder than the last. Every one is **self-verifiable**
(you'll know you did it — no "you should feel more comfortable now") and **reversible** (you
can't break anything). Check them off as you go.

The first three need no setup. The last two come after you've picked a lane.

---

### ⬜ 1. Notice the gap (no setup)
*From [Why beyond chat](./1-why-beyond-chat.md).*

Ask any chat box to "improve this paragraph," pasting a few sentences you wrote.

- **You'll know it worked when:** you can name what the edit was missing — there was no
  *standard* it measured against, no reason to reject a change.

---

### ⬜ 2. Run your first loop (no setup)
*From [Your first win](./2-first-win.md).*

Paste [`autoresearch-writing/SKILL.md`](../../autoresearch-writing/SKILL.md) +
[the example rubric](../../autoresearch-writing/references/rubric.waglesworld-blog.md) + your
draft into a chat box, in chat mode. Let it run.

- **You'll know it worked when:** a dimension score went **up** between turns **and** you
  watched it **revert** a change that didn't help (or tripped an anti-pattern). One KEEP, one
  REVERT.

---

### ⬜ 3. Pick your lane (no setup)
*From [Go deeper](./3-go-deeper.md).*

Decide: do you write code? That's your lane. Read its track page end to end.

- **You'll know it worked when:** you can say which lane you're in and what your first
  lane-skill will be.

---

### ⬜ 4a. Coders — design before you build
*From [Track A](./track-a-cli-agent.md).*

Install a CLI agent, drop in [`brainstorming`](../../brainstorming), and run it before a real
change: "Use the brainstorming skill — I want to build X."

- **You'll know it worked when:** it challenged your premise at least once and produced a short
  design doc *before* any code. A skill ran outside a chat box.

### ⬜ 4b. Knowledge workers — diagnose for real
*From [Track B](./track-b-notes-vault.md).*

Set up an Obsidian vault, then run [`causal`](../../causal) on a real recurring annoyance.

- **You'll know it worked when:** it walked the five-step chain and made you **reject your
  first guess** before landing on a cause. (Bonus: scaffold the vault and run
  [`daily-process`](../../daily-process) on real notes.)

---

### ⬜ 5. Graduate — become an author
*The capstone.*

Stop consuming, start authoring. Either:
- **Customize a rubric:** copy
  [`rubric.example.md`](../../autoresearch-writing/references/rubric.example.md) to
  `rubric.md` and calibrate it to *your* voice using 3–5 of your own pieces, **or**
- **Write a one-screen skill:** a `SKILL.md` with `name`, `description`, and a short procedure
  for a job you do often. (See the [conventions](../../README.md#conventions).)

- **You'll know it worked when:** you ran the loop with *your* rubric, or another person could
  follow your skill and do the job the way you do it. You're authoring, not just consuming.

---

Finished all five? You've gone from "I only chat" to "I run — and write — skills." Consider
contributing yours back: see [CONTRIBUTING.md](../../CONTRIBUTING.md).
