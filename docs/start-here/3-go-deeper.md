# Go deeper

You've felt what a skill does. Now make it part of how you work. There's one fork — and it's
just about what you spend your day on.

## Do you write code?

### → Yes: [Set up a CLI agent](./track-a-cli-agent.md)

An agent that lives in your terminal and *acts* on your project — runs the editing loop on a
real file, ships a PR, debugs a blank page, fetches current API docs. You'll install one
(Claude Code, Codex, or any agent that loads `SKILL.md`) and run a skill end-to-end.

Your starter skills: [`brainstorming`](../../brainstorming) (design before you build),
[`requirements-builder`](../../requirements-builder), then the dev/shipping tier
([`gstack-*`](../../gstack-ship), [`debugging-vite-apps`](../../debugging-vite-apps),
[`get-api-docs`](../../get-api-docs)).

### → No: [Set up a notes vault](./track-b-notes-vault.md)

A place for the model to *think with your knowledge* — process the day's notes, plan your week,
synthesize a series of meetings, clean up a voice journal. You'll set up an Obsidian vault and
run a planning skill on real input.

Your starter skills: [`causal`](../../causal) (diagnose a recurring problem),
[`meeting-synthesizer`](../../meeting-synthesizer) (a great second win — it needs transcripts),
then the notes/planning tier ([`daily-process`](../../daily-process),
[`weekly-plan`](../../weekly-plan), [`journal-process`](../../journal-process), and more).

> [!NOTE]
> Not either/or. Most people do both eventually — the fork is just where to get your *next*
> win fastest. [`autoresearch-writing`](../../autoresearch-writing) serves both lanes, in chat
> mode today and in automated file mode once you have an agent.

---

> [!TIP]
> **Try this:** Pick your lane and complete its first skill on **real input** (a real feature
> you're about to build, or a real annoyance you want diagnosed). Details on the track page.
>
> **You'll know it worked when:** the skill changed your output — it pushed back on your
> premise (coders) or made you reject your first guess about the cause (knowledge workers).
